What Is Go?

Go (also called Golang) is a modern, high-performance programming language created for building fast, scalable, and reliable software. Its simplicity, built-in concurrency model, and strong standard library make it a top choice for backend development, cloud services, distributed systems, and microservices.

Who Created Go?

Go was created at Google by Robert Griesemer, Rob Pike, and Ken Thompson. It was released in 2009 with the goal of combining the performance of C-like languages with the ease and readability of modern programming. Today, Go is widely used by companies like Google, Uber, Netflix, Dropbox, and many others.
